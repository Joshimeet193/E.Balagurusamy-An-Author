About E. Balagurusamy

Erukulla Balagurusamy, born on January 2, 1949, in Namakkal, Tamil Nadu, India, is a distinguished Indian academician, author, and former Member of Parliament (Rajya Sabha). He is renowned for his significant contributions to computer science education and programming languages.

Balagurusamy began his career in academia and quickly rose to prominence as a visionary leader in technical education. He served as the Vice Chancellor of Anna University, Chennai, from 2001 to 2006, where he implemented innovative reforms and initiatives that transformed the university's curriculum and research landscape.

One of his most notable achievements was the development of NEPC (Network Enabled Programming Construct), India's first indigenous programming language designed to facilitate software development in regional languages. This initiative underscored his commitment to bridging the digital divide and promoting inclusive technological advancements across India.

In addition to his academic achievements, Balagurusamy has authored numerous textbooks on computer science and programming that are widely used in educational institutions. His books are known for their clarity, comprehensiveness, and practical approach, making complex concepts accessible to students and professionals alike.

Balagurusamy's contributions to education and technology have earned him several prestigious awards and recognitions over the years. Here are some of the notable awards he has received:

  • Padma Shri - 2002
  • Life Time Achievement Award - 2006, from the Indian Society for Technical Education (ISTE)
  • Distinguished Alumnus Award - 2010, from the Indian Institute of Technology, Roorkee
  • Lifetime Achievement Award - 2012, from the Computer Society of India (CSI)
  • Dr. P. S. Deshmukh Memorial Award - 2015, for outstanding contributions to technical education in India
